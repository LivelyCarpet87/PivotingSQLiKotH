Understanding the Map AND Scoring

The shapes represent servers which you may be able to access. The lines and arrows represent which servers are connected to each other. Arrows represent one-way connections (i.e. if a—>b and you control a, you can attack b but not the other way around). Lines connecting two devices allow connections both ways.

The four circles represent your team servers. You start off with control of one of this server - it is crucial to maintain control, too, as it acts as your ‘entry’ to the rest of the network. Even if you do lose control of it, though, you can always try attacking it again, a feature unique to this server. You gain {{teamOwned}} points per {{scorebotInterval}} seconds that you’re in control of this type of server.

The four ‘diamonds’ represent servers with databases. You start off with control of the ‘diamond’ closest to your team server. The database contains a list of items; you gain {{query}} points for each successful item search in the database conducted by the scorebot every {{scorebotInterval}} seconds in addition to the base of {{db}} points per {{scorebotInterval}} seconds; you can set your own custom filters, but that might decrease the amount of points you get from item searches. The option to completely disable the database grants you zero item search points as expected, and after {{modifierDivider}} seconds the database will be forcefully reenabled to simulate confused disgruntled employees.

The squares represent ‘contested servers’, or servers that are located in between each team server and can attack and be attacked by said team servers as well as their corresponding database servers. They grant {{contested}} points per {{scorebotInterval}} seconds.

The center server is a special snowflake - it can be attacked by 4 contested servers and can attack all four database servers - the most of any server! It grants {{special}} points per {{scorebotInterval}} seconds for the controlling team.

For every {{modifierDivider}} seconds you control any given server, your score multiplier increases by 1, up to a maximum of 10: if you’ve been in control of your team server for 2 hrs and 30 minutes for now, for example, you would currently be getting {{teamOwned}} * (1 + 2) = 60 points per {{scorebotInterval}} seconds.

Filters

Filters are your defenses. Each time someone tries to log in, the server checks their username and password against the filters. If any filtered string of characters is found in the username or password, the server will ignore the attempt to log in. The same applies for database queries. Filters ignore upper/lower case and spaces when comparing strings, so filter "abc" is going to match "0Ab Cd".

If you initially owned the device (name starts with your team letter and ends with 1 or 2, eg a1), you can add as many filters as you want. Otherwise, you may only add 1 filter item to EACH filter.

Nighttime Pause

To promote healthy amounts of sleeping, the game pauses from 22:30 to 7:45 the following day. During this period of pause, players cannot access game elements, the scoreboard, the map, tutorials, or hints. As expected, the scorebot will also be paused and scores/modifiers will not increment. This was done to prevent players from staying up too late or feel penalized for sleeping sanely.
